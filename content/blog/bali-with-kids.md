## Choosing the Right Base: The Best Family Places in Bali

Your experience will heavily depend on where you choose to unpack your bags. Bali is diverse, and each neighborhood offers a completely different vibe. When scouting for the **best family places in bali**, you need to consider traffic, walkability, and proximity to amenities.

**Sanur: The Calm and Stroller-Friendly Haven**
Sanur is often our top recommendation for families with toddlers. Located on the east coast, it boasts a 5-kilometer paved beach path that is entirely free of motorbikes. It is one of the few places in Bali where you can comfortably use a stroller. The water here is protected by a reef, creating calm, shallow pools perfect for little swimmers.

**Canggu: For the Trendy Digital Nomad Family**
If you have older kids or need top-tier coworking spaces, Canggu is the place to be. While the traffic can be intense, Canggu offers the highest concentration of trendy cafes, indoor playgrounds, and international schools. It is undeniably one of the **best family places in bali** if you want to seamlessly blend remote work with an active social life.

**Ubud: Nature and Culture Immersion**
Nestled in the jungle, Ubud is the cultural heart of Bali. It is ideal for families looking to escape the beach heat and immerse themselves in traditional arts, yoga, and nature walks.

## Finding the Perfect Family Accommodation

When you are committing to a 30-day stay, cramped hotel rooms quickly lose their charm. For an extended trip, we always opt for a private villa. Having a full kitchen to prepare familiar snacks and a private pool for afternoon cooldowns makes a world of difference for family harmony.

As we put together **A Month in Bali with Kids: The Ultimate Guide**, accommodation safety was our number one priority.

**Top Tips for Villa Rentals:**

- **The Pool Fence Rule:** Do not assume a villa is child-safe. Most private pools in Bali drop straight off the living room with no barrier. However, you can easily hire local companies to install a temporary bamboo or glass pool fence before you arrive.
- **Verify the Wi-Fi:** If you plan to work remotely, do not just take the host's word for it. Ask them to send a screenshot of a recent speed test.
- **Enclosed Living Spaces:** Many traditional Balinese villas feature open-air living rooms. While beautiful, they invite mosquitoes and tropical heat. Look for "enclosed living" villas with air conditioning in all bedrooms and common areas.

## Top 10 Things to Do in Bali with Family

Bali is a massive outdoor playground. To keep the kids entertained without burning out, you need a mix of high-energy activities and relaxed cultural experiences. If you are searching for the **top 10 things to do in bali with family**, this curated list covers all ages.

**1. Splash Around at Waterbom Bali**
Consistently rated as the best waterpark in Asia, Waterbom in Kuta is a masterclass in family entertainment. It is spotlessly clean, heavily shaded by tropical gardens, and features rides ranging from gentle lazy rivers to adrenaline-pumping slides.

**2. Explore the Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary**
Located in Ubud, this lush forest is home to hundreds of long-tailed macaques. It is a massive hit with kids, but a word of warning: these monkeys are cheeky. Keep a close eye on your belongings, remove sunglasses, and never bring food into the forest.

**3. Enjoy Calm Beach Days in Nusa Dua**
While the west coast is famous for surfing, the beaches in Nusa Dua offer pristine white sand and incredibly calm waters. It is the perfect spot for building sandcastles and letting toddlers splash safely.

**4. Visit the Bali Safari and Marine Park**
A fantastic day out where you can take a safari journey in an air-conditioned tram to see animals from Indonesia, India, and Africa roaming in large enclosures.

![Children looking at the lush green jungle pathways inside the Sacred Monkey Forest in Ubud](/images/blog/ubud-monkey-forest.webp)

**5. Release Hatchlings at the Turtle Conservation Center**
Located on Serangan Island, this center rescues injured sea turtles and protects eggs. Kids can learn about marine conservation and, depending on the season, help release baby turtles back into the ocean.

**6. Walk the Tegalalang Rice Terraces**
A stunning UNESCO World Heritage site. Get there early in the morning to beat the heat and the crowds. The kids will love navigating the narrow dirt paths winding through the vibrant green paddies.

**7. Relax at Family-Friendly Beach Clubs**
Not all beach clubs are wild party zones. Places like Ku De Ta or Sundays Beach Club are incredibly welcoming to families during the day, offering great food, safe swimming, and a relaxed atmosphere.

**8. Watch the Uluwatu Kecak Fire Dance**
Perched on a cliff at sunset, this traditional performance uses no instruments—just the hypnotic chanting of dozens of men. The dramatic fire displays and monkey characters will keep older kids completely mesmerized.

**9. Take a Family Cooking Class**
Balinese cuisine is delicious and accessible. Many organic farms in Ubud offer family cooking classes where you start by picking fresh ingredients from the garden before learning to make simple, kid-approved dishes like _Mie Goreng_.

**10. Hike the Campuhan Ridge Walk**
A relatively easy, paved walk in Ubud that offers sweeping views of the lush valleys. It is one of the best **top 10 things to do in bali with family** to burn off some morning energy before it gets too hot.

## Balancing Work and Play: Remote Work in Paradise

One of the main reasons we created **A Month in Bali with Kids: The Ultimate Guide** is because Bali is the ultimate digital nomad destination. You do not have to put your career on hold to give your kids a tropical adventure.

The internet infrastructure across the island is generally excellent, especially in hubs like Canggu, Sanur, and Ubud. But the real magic lies in the ecosystem built around remote-working parents.

There are countless **kid friendly places in bali** designed specifically for this lifestyle. You will find dozens of cafes featuring high-speed Wi-Fi in the front and supervised, air-conditioned kids' clubs in the back. Furthermore, hiring a local nanny (often referred to as a _pembantu_ or nanny) is highly affordable and common practice. Balinese nannies are renowned for their loving, attentive nature, allowing you to focus on your Zoom calls with total peace of mind.

## Essential Survival Tips for a Month in Bali with Kids

Living in the tropics requires a slight adjustment period. To ensure your 30-day stay goes off without a hitch, keep these practical tips in mind.

**Navigating Transportation**
Do not try to navigate Bali's chaotic traffic in a rental car on day one. Instead, download the **Gojek** or **Grab** apps. They are the Southeast Asian equivalents of Uber. You can order cheap, reliable cars for family outings, or even have groceries and medicine delivered directly to your villa in under an hour.

**Conquering "Bali Belly"**
Stomach bugs happen, especially when kids accidentally swallow pool or shower water. Only drink bottled or filtered water—never tap water. Pack activated charcoal and probiotics from home. If anyone gets sick, excellent medical care is available. BIMC Hospital offers international-standard pediatric care.

**Visa Requirements**
For a one-month stay, the standard Visa on Arrival (VOA) is perfect. It grants you 30 days and can be extended once for another 30 days. To skip the long immigration lines at the airport, apply for the e-VOA online before you fly.

## FAQ

**Is Bali safe for young kids and toddlers?**
Yes, Bali is incredibly safe for young children. The local culture highly values family, and Balinese people are wonderfully affectionate toward kids. Your main safety concerns will simply be sun protection, mosquito bite prevention, and ensuring you rent a villa with a secure pool fence.

**What are the most kid friendly places in bali for dining out?**
You will find endless **kid friendly places in bali**. Cafes like Milk & Madu in Canggu or Shotgun Social in Sanur offer fantastic menus alongside massive outdoor playgrounds, bouncy castles, and weekend face painting.

**Do I need to bring a stroller or a baby carrier?**
Bring a baby carrier. Outside of Sanur and the Nusa Dua resort areas, Bali’s sidewalks are often broken, narrow, or non-existent. A comfortable, breathable baby carrier is essential for navigating places like Canggu or Ubud.

**How much does it cost to spend a month in Bali with a family?**
Costs vary wildly based on your lifestyle. You can rent a comfortable 2-bedroom villa for $1,500 - $3,000 USD a month. Eating at local warungs is very cheap, while western cafes cost similar to back home. Overall, it is highly affordable compared to Western countries.
